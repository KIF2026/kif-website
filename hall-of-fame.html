Honoring those whose legacy, character, and influence continue to inspire American Kenpo and the Kenpo International Federation.

In Memoriam

Sensei Miguel Vélez

Sensei Miguel Vélez, though he passed before the founding of the Kenpo International Federation (KIF), remains a foundational figure in its spirit and legacy. A dedicated and highly respected Kenpo practitioner, he made significant contributions to North Hollywood Kenpo Karate—an institution that would later evolve into KIF.

“You were not just a friend, you were my master.”

Beyond martial arts, Sensei Miguel Vélez was known as a devoted son, husband, father, and friend—an exceptional sensei whose impact continues to be felt by all who had the privilege of knowing him.

Living Inductees

Sensei Rigoberto Samayoa

Sensei Rigoberto Samayoa is honored as the second inductee into the Kenpo International Federation (KIF) Hall of Fame. A distinguished martial artist, he built an impressive legacy in Guatemala and played a pivotal role in the development of American Kenpo by awarding Jorge Mario Calvillo his first black belt in Kenpo Karate.

Sensei Mohamad Tabatabai

Sensei Mohamad Tabatabai is honored as a distinguished inductee into the Kenpo International Federation (KIF) Hall of Fame. He earned his black belt directly from Grand Master Ed Parker and has built a remarkable legacy as one of the most influential figures in the art.
